Why Register?

You can reserve a screen name for any of the JNCO chatrooms by registering a screen name of your choice. Registering that name with us means that for the next 30 days, no one else can use that name. This eliminates the problem of "posers".

Do I Have To Register?

No. You do not have to register any name to participate in any of the JNCO chat rooms.

How Do I Change My Registered Name?

Currently there are 2 ways to change your name:

1) Simple: Just fool your browser into thinking you are in another chat room. To do this use an extra '/' somewhere in the address you use to get to our chat room. For example, instead of going to http://www.jnco.com/chat, go to http://www.jnco.com//chat. Your browser will think that it is at a different address, and not tell the chat room who you are, allowing you to re-register if you wish.

2) More difficult: Destroy the cookie your browser uses to identify you. This would prevent you from using your old name for a couple of weeks until I clean the name out of the registry database.

To do this just click erase screen name.

There is another one located at www.jnco.com/chat/chatgroove/killCookie.cgi for any cookie you may have set there. After visiting the Cookie Eater(s), close your browser, and restart it. If you see your old name still there, hit the refresh button before posting a message.

Finally, if you still have trouble with either of these methods, go to www.jnco.com/chat/chatgroove, and ask somebody there to help you. Frequently, the users there are savvy enough to know these "secret" tricks.

Why Does the Graffifi Wall Loose My Registration?

You seem to have a promiscuous browser. To register, it must accept a "cookie" from the chat room. That is a little bit of information that your browser stores on your hard drive. Whenever it accesses the graffiti wall, it then gives the cookie back to the chat room. The chat room reads the cookie and learns who you are. The problem happens when the browser deletes the cookie. The reason is usually because most browsers refuse to accept more than 300 cookies. Some roughish pages will send you as many as 7 or more cookies per page. It your cookie quota is full, your browser will delete some of your old cookies to make room for the new ones. Then, when you go back to the graffiti wall, no cookie, no registration. The wall knows that somebody registered under the old name, and will not let anybody else do it.

The solution: Set you browsers options to ask you permission before accepting cookies. Then, only accept cookies that you want, and do not accept cookies from sites that only want to study your browsing habits.

Use the escape button or click "no" before accepting cookies that have nothing to do with anything you want. In any event, your privacy is important, and these businesses and government pages should not be tracking your browsing habits.

Okay, a cacheing problem may also be to blame. Just hit the refresh button, and the page would give you back your old name if that is the case.
